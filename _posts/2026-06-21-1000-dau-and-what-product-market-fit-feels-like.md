Big day. BoundRift crossed 1000 daily active users, driven by a large regional tournament that brought a lot of people to the site at once. Reddit and SEO have been doing quiet work in the background, and it showed today.

The caveat I keep in mind is that ecosystem projects for games are inherently fragile - the game has to stay healthy, the community has to stay engaged, and a lot of things outside my control have to keep going right. So I'm not treating this as a signal to change the nature of the project. It's still a hobby project, and keeping that framing feels important.

What it does feel like is product market fit in the purest sense - people finding something useful and coming back for it. There are incumbents in this space and I'm aware of them trying to adapt, but I think the refinement I've put into BoundRift is real and I'm hopeful that feature completeness and continued improvement will do the work that aggressive marketing can't. The constraint I'm watching is not stretching too thin. The product surface is already large and I'd rather do fewer things well than lose the quality that's brought people here.

The part I keep coming back to is what this feels like personally. I've spent the last six years in product and executive roles, directing work and thinking about strategy, and somewhere in that time I stopped feeling like an engineer. Working on BoundRift has brought that back - the technical problem solving, the ownership over the full stack, learning DevOps and cost optimization and performance tuning not because someone assigned it but because I need it. Optimizing the software to run faster and cheaper and more smoothly while watching real users benefit from it is deeply satisfying in a way that's different from anything I've done in a leadership role.

Part of what makes it feel different from past work is the absence of VC expectations. There's no pressure to grow at a specific rate or justify the roadmap to anyone. I'm building things I'm excited about for users who are excited to use them, and that feedback loop is its own reward. Whether it stays that way or becomes something more is a question for later.
